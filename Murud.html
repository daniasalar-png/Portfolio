Murud-Janjira Fort

Murud-Janjira is a strong sea fort located on an island off the coast of Maharashtra. It is one of the most powerful forts in India.

The fort is famous for never being conquered despite multiple attacks. It has strong walls, cannons, and secret entrances.

Murud-Janjira is an architectural marvel and a must-visit historical site.

The architecture of Murud-Janjira Fort, often called the "Invincible Fort," is a masterclass in naval defense and strategic deception. Built on an oval-shaped rock island in the Arabian Sea, its massive basalt walls rise directly from the water to a height of about 40 feet. One of its most ingenious features is the main entrance, which is cleverly hidden behind a large bastion and only becomes visible when a boat is within 40 feet of the fort. This design effectively thwarted direct naval assaults for centuries, as enemy fleets would struggle to locate the entry point while under heavy fire from the fort’s 26 rounded bastions.
