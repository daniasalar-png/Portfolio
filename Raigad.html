Raigad Fort

Raigad Fort was the capital of the Maratha Empire under Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. It is located in the Sahyadri mountain range in Maharashtra.

The fort is most famous for Shivaji Maharaj’s coronation in 1674. It features strong fortifications, massive gates, and secret pathways.

The fort also includes the royal palace, market area, and Shivaji Maharaj’s samadhi. Raigad remains a symbol of courage, leadership, and Maratha pride.

The architecture of Raigad Fort is a masterpiece of strategic planning, designed by Hiroji Indulkar. Perched 820 metres above sea level, the fort is accessible via a single path, as the other three sides are surrounded by sheer vertical cliffs. Its massive entrance, the Maha Darwaja, is flanked by two bastions and was built to be invisible to enemies until they were right upon it. Within the walls, the Raj Sabha (Royal Court) showcases advanced acoustics, designed so that a whisper at the entrance could be heard clearly by the King sitting on his throne at the far end.
