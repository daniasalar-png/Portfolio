Pratapgad Fort

Pratapgad Fort is located near Mahabaleshwar and is famous for the historic battle between Shivaji Maharaj and Afzal Khan in 1659.

The fort is surrounded by dense forests and has strong defensive walls. It played a major role in the expansion of the Maratha Empire.

Pratapgad is an important tourist destination and stands as a symbol of strategic brilliance.

The architecture of Pratapgad Fort, completed in 1656, is a masterclass in defensive strategy designed by Hiroji Indulkar under the orders of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. Located in the Satara district of Maharashtra, approximately 24 kilometres from Mahabaleshwar, the fort stands at an elevation of about 1,080 metres (3,543 feet). It is uniquely divided into two sections: the Upper Fort, built on a rugged hilltop with sheer cliffs dropping up to 250 metres, and the Lower Fort, which features high walls and bastions over 10 metres tall to guard the Par Pass and nearby river valleys.
