Sinhagad Fort

Sinhagad Fort is located near Pune and is known for its historical importance. It was the site of the famous battle led by Tanaji Malusare.

The fort stands on a steep hill and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys. It played a key role in Maratha history.

Today, Sinhagad is a popular trekking destination and attracts many visitors. It reflects bravery, sacrifice, and strength.

The architecture of Sinhagad Fort, originally known as Kondhana, is a testament to 2,000 years of strategic military engineering. Standing at an elevation of 1,312 metres in the Sahyadri mountains, the fort's design takes full advantage of its natural topography, featuring steep slopes and sheer basalt cliffs that act as formidable barriers against invaders. It is accessible through two main historical gateways: the Pune Darwaza, which faces the city of Pune, and the Kalyan Darwaza, which opens toward the Konkan region. Both gates are strategically positioned and showcase intricate stonework designed to slow and funnel potential attackers.
